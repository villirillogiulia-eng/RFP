# Mudug School Risk & Displacement Report (2024 Update)
## Prepared for: EBI Education Continuity Project

### 1. Executive Summary
This enhanced report analyzes the vulnerability of educational facilities in the Mudug region using a **Weighted Risk Intensity Score**. Unlike simple proximity analysis, this model factors in conflict severity (deaths), recency (2024 vs 2023), and cumulative pressure from multiple nearby events.

*   **Total Schools Assessed:** 47
*   **High Risk (Weighted / ≤10km):** 10
*   **Medium Risk (Weighted / 11-25km):** 3
*   **Low Risk (Weighted / >25km):** 34
*   **Regional Displacement:** 70,882 IDPs (OCHA 2022)

---

### 2. Weighted Risk Methodology
The **Risk Intensity Score** is calculated as:
`Score = Σ (Severity * RecencyWeight) / (Distance + 1)²`
*   **Severity:** Total estimated deaths per event.
*   **RecencyWeight:** 1.5 for 2024 events; 1.0 for 2023 events.
*   **Distance:** Schools within 50km of any conflict event are assessed.

### 4. Planning & Displacement Recommendations
1.  **IDP Integration:** With 70,882 IDPs in Mudug, low-risk schools must be assessed for their capacity to absorb displaced students.
2.  **Severity-Based Protection:** Schools near high-mortality conflict zones (e.g., Harardhere district) require "Safe School" protocols and teacher emergency training.
3.  **Dynamic Monitoring:** The dashboard should be updated weekly to track shifting "Hotspots" as new conflict events are recorded.
